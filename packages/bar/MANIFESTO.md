# You Built Something. Now Understand It.

You shipped an app. Maybe you prompted your way through it. Maybe you followed a tutorial and adapted it. Maybe you just kept asking Claude or ChatGPT until the errors stopped and the thing worked. However you got here — it works, it's deployed, and that matters.

But there's a gap between "I shipped a thing" and "I understand the thing I shipped."

You see files like `tsconfig.json` and `next.config.ts` and you're not sure what they do. You know your app uses React but you couldn't explain what a server component is. You've got a `package.json` with 30 dependencies and you didn't choose any of them. Your AI did.

That's okay. Seriously. That's not a failure — that's a starting point.

## What This Tool Does

VibeLens is a tiny component you add to your app. It puts a bar at the bottom of your page. Click it and you get:

- **A plain-English summary** of what your app does and how it works
- **A stack guide** that explains every technology in your project — what React is, what TypeScript does, why you have a `postcss.config.js` — in words that actually make sense
- **A technical walkthrough** of how all the pieces fit together
- **An interactive quiz** that tests whether you actually understand your own project (you might be surprised)
- **A friendly code review** that points out what to fix, with links to learn why

All of this is generated by AI from your source code. You copy one prompt, paste it into your AI tool of choice, and it writes the docs for you. Everything stays on your machine. No accounts. No API keys. No data leaves your computer.

## Why This Exists

Vibe coding is real. People are building real products — SaaS tools, portfolios, side projects, startup MVPs — by describing what they want and letting AI write the code. And it works. The code runs. The app deploys. Users sign up.

But there's a ceiling. When something breaks, you can't debug it because you don't know how it works. When you want to add a feature, you don't know where to put it. When someone asks you about your tech stack in an interview, you don't know what to say.

The fix isn't "go take a computer science course." The fix is learning in context — understanding YOUR code, YOUR project, YOUR stack. Not abstract concepts from a textbook, but the specific tools and patterns that are already in your `node_modules`.

That's what this tool does. It turns your codebase into a classroom.

## Our Beliefs

**Vibe coding is valid.** You built something that works. That's not nothing. That's the hardest part of learning to code — getting something real into the world. Everything after that is refinement.

**Understanding should be effortless.** You shouldn't have to leave your app, open a course, watch a video, and then try to map what you learned back to your code. The explanations should be right there, inside the thing you built.

**AI helped you build it. Let AI help you understand it.** The same tools that wrote your code can explain your code. We just give them the right prompts.

**Learning is not linear.** You don't need to learn HTML before CSS before JavaScript before React before Next.js. You already have a Next.js app. Start there. Work backwards. Understand what you have, then fill in the gaps.

**No judgment.** We don't care if you wrote every line by hand or if Claude wrote all of it. We care that you want to understand it. That impulse — "wait, how does this actually work?" — is the beginning of real engineering.

## How It Works

1. Install the package
2. Add one component to your layout
3. Click "Generate Documents" in the bar
4. Copy the prompt, paste it into ChatGPT/Claude/your AI tool
5. Save the files it generates
6. Learn about your project

That's it. No accounts. No configuration. No API keys. Everything runs locally, everything stays private.

## Who This Is For

- **Junior developers** onboarding onto a new codebase
- **Hobbyists** who are curious about the technologies in their projects
